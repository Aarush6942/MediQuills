Our Team

Meet the dedicated individuals working to advance scientific literacy and vaccine access worldwide.

Ridhi Gutta - President

Ridhi Gutta is a rising junior in high school and a passionate advocate for girls' empowerment in STEM and entrepreneurship. She is a 4x #1 Bestselling Author, with her books ranking among over 32.8 million titles globally, and is recognized as an internationally acclaimed researcher and entrepreneur.

Sydney Yerra - Vice President

Sydney Yerra is a rising junior with a love for creativity, collaboration, and community impact. A dedicated viola player and curious thinker, she brings both artistic expression and sharp insight to everything she does. Sydney is deeply passionate about making science more accessible and believes storytelling has the power to spark change.

Audrey Huynh

Audrey is a senior in high school from the Bay Area in California, and she hopes to continue exploring the medical field through research and service as she pursues medical school. In her free time, she loves to read and learn new hobbies, such as crocheting. She is very excited to be working with MediQuills to promote its purpose!

Kate Russel - Director Of Literacy

Kate Russell is a high school sophomore with a passion for how youth scientific literacy can promote public trust in science and equal access to scientific resources. She is interested in biotechnology and biochemistry and enjoys reading, creative writing, and debate in her free time.

Ian Kim - Co Director of Policy

Ian Kim, a 10th grader from McLean, Virginia, attends Phillips Academy in Andover, Massachusetts. Combining his love for writing with a passion for biochemistry, he authored a book that raised over $2,000 for the Gift of Life organ donor program. As director of policy, he aims to advocate for greater youth involvement in international science policy with the UN and EU & build a more inclusive scientific community.

Keshvee Sekhda - Co Director of Policy

Keshvee is a curious and driven student passionate about innovation, research, and using technology to solve real-world problems. Whether mentoring younger students in robotics or exploring the intersections of science and society through projects, she strives to create impact through collaboration and creativity!
